## 2. CORE PHILOSOPHY & APPROACH

### What Makes NTA Different
NTA positions itself against mainstream, one-size-fits-all nutrition. Their approach is built on:

1. **Bio-Individuality** -- "Each person is biologically unique." No universal diet prescription. Each client is addressed differently according to their unique needs.

2. **Food-First Approach** -- Rooted in preparation techniques of traditional cultures. Whole, thoughtfully prepared, nutrient-dense foods are the primary intervention.

3. **Omnivorous Perspective** -- They take an explicitly omnivorous perspective on diet (not vegan, not keto, not any single dietary framework).

4. **Innate Intelligence** -- "The body possesses innate intelligence and defaults to health when given what it needs." They honor the body's capacity to restore, regenerate, and correct itself.

5. **Root-Cause Focus** -- They don't chase symptoms. They seek to understand WHY the body responds a certain way.

6. **Whole-Person/Holistic Lens** -- Support the entire body as a network of systems rather than addressing specific symptoms in isolation.

7. **Foundations-Based** -- Everything is organized around physiological foundations (see below).

## 3. THE FIVE FOUNDATIONS OF HEALTH

This is the core framework of the NTP curriculum. Everything revolves around these five areas:

### 1. Nutrient-Dense Diet
"Whole, thoughtfully prepared foods are a source of joy and nourish the body."
- Emphasis on food quality, preparation methods, traditional cultures
- Nutrient density over calorie counting

### 2. Digestion
"What we eat has little impact if we can't digest and absorb the nutrients."
- North-to-south digestive process
- Absorption and assimilation focus

### 3. Blood Sugar Regulation
Essential for metabolism, inflammation, hormones, and mental health.
- Impact on energy, mood, weight, hormonal balance

### 4. Sleep
"Adequate, quality sleep is essential for optimal physiological and cognitive function."
- Recognized as a physiological foundation, not just a lifestyle preference

### 5. Stress Management
"Managing our response to stress has profoundly positive impacts on health."
- Stress response, adrenal function, cortisol

**Note:** Some pages list "Six Physiological Foundations" adding Bio-Individuality as the sixth. The NTP curriculum page lists five. The about page lists six. Bio-individuality is both a foundation AND an overarching principle.

- **Credential Earned:** NTP (Nutritional Therapy Practitioner)
- **Board Exam:** Graduates can sit for NANP's National Board Exam
- **Live Calls:** Weekly, two time slot options
- **Platform:** Canvas LMS

### 4b. Functional Nutritional Therapy Practitioner (FNTP)
- NOT a separate program -- it's a credential upgrade earned by completing the FCA course after NTP
- FNTPs can perform hands-on Functional Clinical Assessments
- FNTPs can utilize Lingual-Neural Testing (LNT)

### 4c. Professional Health & Wellness Coach (PHWC) Program
- **Duration:** 8 months, fully online, instructor-led
- **Tuition:** $5,000 (before promotions)
- **Time Commitment:** 8-10 hours/week (some pages say 5-10, some say 10-15)
- **Prerequisites:** High school diploma/GED, 18+
- **Credential Earned:** NTA Certified PHWC
- **Board Exam:** NBHWC HWC Certifying Examination (additional requirements: 50 coaching sessions + Associates degree or 4,000 hours work experience)
- **Cohorts:** Spring and Fall
- **Platform:** Canvas LMS

**PHWC Definition:** "Health coaching is the creative process that empowers a client to make lasting behavior changes and regain confidence in their ability to succeed."

**PHWC Curriculum (8 months):**
- Month 1: Introduction to Coaching, Client-Centered Relationship, Active Listening, Client Emotions
- Month 2: Expand Conversation, Reflections, Coach Prep, Goals & Action
- Month 3: Client Intake, Initial Session, Follow-up Sessions, Mentor Sessions, Focus/Refocus
- Month 4: Freedom of Choice, Awareness/Perspective Shifts, Self-Efficacy, Coaching Models
- Month 5: Active Experimentation, Positive Psychological Resources, Final Session
- Month 6: Professional Conduct, Ethics & Legal
- Month 7: Health Promotion, Chronic Disease, Health Information, Behavioral Risk Factors
- Month 8: Practical Skills Assessment, Final Exam, Graduation

**PHWC Foundations of Coaching:**
1. Client-Centered
2. Bio-Individuality
3. Trust
4. Resilience
5. Ethics

**Key PHWC Tools:**
- NTA Health and Wellness Wheel Assessment
- Motivational interviewing
- Behavior change psychology frameworks
- Nutri-Q software (different features than NTP)

**NTP vs PHWC (NTA's own framing):**
- NTP focuses on Foundations of Health, determining food/lifestyle/supplement recommendations for bio-individual needs
- PHWC focuses on skills and techniques for sustainable, lasting behavior change
- "An NTP seeks to fulfill the role of a health and nutrition expert; a PHWC seeks to offer support, encouragement, and reflection that empowers a client to truly own the direction and outcomes of their wellness journey"
- NTP = data-driven, science-focused, nutrition expertise
- PHWC = empathy-driven, behavior change, coaching psychology

### 4d. Functional Clinical Assessment (FCA) Course
- **Duration:** 9-week online course + 3-day in-person workshop
- **Cost:** $2,000
- **Capacity:** 22 spots (limited)
- **Prerequisite:** Must be NTP graduate with active credential
- **Credential Earned:** FNTP (Functional Nutritional Therapy Practitioner)

**FCA Curriculum:**
- Body mechanics
- Reflex point anatomy
- Lingual-Neural Testing (LNT)
- Palpation techniques
- Data analysis
- Coca's Pulse Test
- Nutrient recommendations
- Professional conduct

**What FCA Teaches:**
- Hands-on technique to communicate directly with the body through targeted palpation
- Assessment of reflex points to identify areas of organ stress and dysfunction
- Lingual-Neural Testing -- evaluates body's neurological response to specific substances placed on the tongue
- Creates highly personalized, validated protocols

### 4f. Career Compass

## 5. SCOPE OF PRACTICE

### NTP Scope -- CAN do:
- Evaluate intake information for customized recommendations
- Promote nutrient-dense foods with emphasis on variety and balance
- Focus on Foundations of Nutrition to optimize body's balance
- Educate on direct-to-consumer health tools (lab testing, wearables)
- Refer clients to licensed medical professionals
- Food-first approach with a holistic lens
- Honor bio-individuality through whole-person perspective

### NTP Scope -- CANNOT do:
- Practice Medical Nutrition Therapy (MNT)
- Diagnose or treat disease
- Make unproven health claims
- Prescribe or cure any pathological condition
- State or imply state licensure/certification
- Work with medical/mental health conditions without referral

### FNTP Additional Scope:
- Conduct hands-on Functional Clinical Assessments
- Utilize Lingual-Neural Testing to assess which nutrients/supplements best suit clients

### PHWC Scope -- CAN do:
- Review intake information for readiness assessment
- Support clients in exploring values and purpose
- Empower clients to design their own action steps
- Foster resilience and growth mindset
- Partner with clients' medical teams on prescribed protocols
- Refer to licensed professionals
- Work in private practice or healthcare settings

### PHWC Scope -- CANNOT do:
- Recommend food, supplements, or lifestyle habits (key distinction from NTP)
- Direct or determine client goals
- Diagnose or treat disease
- Make unproven health claims
- Misrepresent training or title

**Critical Distinction:** NTPs make recommendations. PHWCs empower clients to make their own choices. "The client is the expert in their own life."
