## NTA Overview
<!-- source_url: https://www.nutritionaltherapy.com/about -->

### Mission and Vision

The Nutritional Therapy Association's mission is to reverse the epidemic of preventable poor health by educating and empowering wellness champions in every community. The NTA's vision is to lead a wellness revolution that creates an alternative system of care, prioritizing people over profit with a network of professionals who provide the knowledge, resources, and support necessary to reclaim health. The NTA positions itself as a response to a growing health crisis: 74 percent of adults are overweight or obese, 60 percent of people battle chronic disease, one in three people have pre-diabetes, and 50 percent of children and adolescents are overweight or obese. The organization frames its educational programs as the mechanism for training practitioners who can address these challenges at the community level through bio-individualized, food-first nutritional therapy.

### History — Founded 1997 by Gray and Joy Graham

The Nutritional Therapy Association was incorporated in 1997 by Gray and Joy Graham as a vocational nutrition school to train healthcare practitioners in nutritional therapy. The organization is headquartered in Olympia, Washington. The NTP program was launched in 2001, certifying the first cohorts of Functional Nutritional Therapy Practitioners and Nutritional Therapy Practitioners. In 2012, the National Association of Nutritional Professionals (NANP) included NTA programs in its list of Approved and Recommended Holistic Nutrition Education Programs. In 2023, the Grahams established the Graham Grant, a financial assistance program that reduces NTP tuition by 50 percent for qualifying low-income applicants. In 2024, the NTA launched its Professional Health and Wellness Coach (PHWC) program, which received approval from the National Board for Health and Wellness Coaching (NBHWC). Over its 25-plus year history, the NTA has graduated more than 10,000 practitioners.

### Stats and Impact

The NTA cites several key impact metrics that reflect its scope and influence. Over 10,000 graduates have completed NTA programs since the organization's founding. The NTA has operated continuously for more than 25 years, making it one of the longest-established holistic nutrition schools in the United States. The organization's tagline, reversing the trend one practitioner at a time, reflects its theory of change: training individual practitioners who collectively create systemic impact across communities. Graduates work in private practice, healthcare settings, corporate wellness, education, and public health advocacy across the country and internationally.

### Leadership Team

The NTA's leadership includes Michael Belz as Chief Executive Officer. The Board of Directors consists of Grayson Graham serving as Board Chair, Sharon Weinberg, and Kristin Rowell. Academic leadership is led by Mallory Acosta, FNTP, as Vice President of Academics; Krista McCafferty, NTP, BCHN, as Curriculum Manager; and McKay Bennett, NBC-HWC, NTP, as PHWC Program Director. Jamie Belz, FNTP and Master Health Coach, serves as the host of the NTA's Body Wisdom Podcast and is positioned as a health freedom advocate within the organization's brand.

### Accreditation and Licensing

The NTA is a private vocational school in Washington State, licensed by the Washington State Workforce Training and Education Coordinating Board. The NTA's programs are recognized by the National Association of Nutritional Professionals (NANP), and NTP graduates can sit for the NANP National Board Exam. The PHWC program is an Approved Health and Wellness Coach Training and Education Program by the National Board for Health and Wellness Coaching (NBHWC). The NTA is endorsed by the Holistic Council. It is important to note that the NTA is not accredited by the U.S. Department of Education, which means NTA programs do not qualify for federal financial aid. This does not affect the validity of NTA credentials for professional practice but may be relevant for students who are comparing educational options or seeking transferable academic credits.

## Nutritional Therapy Practitioner (NTP) Program
<!-- source_url: https://www.nutritionaltherapy.com/ntp -->

### Program Summary — Duration, Cost, Credential

The NTP program is a 12-month, fully online, instructor-led certification course. Tuition is approximately $8,000 before any promotions or financial assistance. Upon successful completion, graduates earn the NTP (Nutritional Therapy Practitioner) credential. The program requires approximately 15 to 20 hours per week of study time, including live calls, reading, assignments, and community group calls. Three cohorts are offered per year with start dates in February, June, and October. Weekly live calls are 90 minutes long with two time slot options per cohort.

### Prerequisites and Admissions

The NTP program requires a high school diploma or GED equivalent and a minimum age of 18 years. Minors may request an exemption but cannot receive certification until they turn 18. No prior nutrition, science, or healthcare background is required. The program is designed to take students from foundational knowledge through clinical competency within the 12-month curriculum. Enrollment is rolling, with students applying for their preferred cohort start date.

### What You Learn — Curriculum Overview

The NTP curriculum consists of 15 modules organized into three tiers. Foundational knowledge modules cover food relationship and practices, anatomy and physiology, and basics of nutrition. Foundation deep dive modules explore each of the Five Foundations of Health in detail: nutrient-dense diet, digestion, blood sugar regulation, sleep, and stress management. Clinical and applied practice modules cover the case study intensive, supplements, the NACA process (including client paperwork, practitioner forms, and Nutri-Q software), practice client work, and functional lab testing. Students gain proficiency with assessment tools including the Nutritional Assessment Questionnaire (NAQ), Symptom Burden Graph, Food and Mood Journals, and the Nutri-Q client management platform.

### What You Can Do After Graduation

NTP graduates can work as nutrition professionals providing customized dietary, supplement, and lifestyle recommendations to support clients in achieving their health goals. Career paths include private practice with one-on-one clients, group coaching programs, wellness workshops, corporate wellness facilitation, allied practice alongside healthcare providers, community health advocacy, thought leadership through content creation, and education roles. NTPs can work independently in most states, though regulatory requirements vary by jurisdiction and practitioners must verify their state's specific rules.

### Board Exam and Professional Recognition

NTP graduates are eligible to sit for the NANP National Board Exam, which is the primary board certification for holistic nutrition practitioners in the United States. Passing the NANP board exam provides an additional nationally recognized credential. The NTP credential is also recognized by the Holistic Council. NTP graduates can pursue advanced training through the Functional Clinical Assessment (FCA) course to earn the FNTP credential, which adds hands-on assessment capabilities.

### Tuition and Financial Options

NTP tuition is approximately $8,000, with early bird discounts and promotional pricing periodically available. The NTA offers financing through Affirm, allowing students to spread payments over time. The NTA also offers periodic tuition reduction promotions throughout the year.

### The Graham Grant — 50 Percent Tuition Reduction

The Graham Grant was established in 2023 by NTA founders Joy and Gray Graham to increase access to NTP education for students who face financial barriers. The grant reduces NTP tuition by 50 percent, bringing the cost to approximately $4,000. Eligibility is limited to U.S. residents who meet low-income thresholds as defined by Office of Postsecondary Education guidelines. Applicants must submit Form 1040 tax returns to verify income eligibility. The Graham Grant cannot be combined with other promotional discounts. Applicants must not be existing NTP graduates. Awards are first-come, first-served with a limited supply per cohort, and each award is valid for 7 business days from issuance.

## Professional Health and Wellness Coach (PHWC) Program
<!-- source_url: https://www.nutritionaltherapy.com/health-coach -->

### Program Summary — Duration, Cost, Credential

The PHWC program is an 8-month, fully online, instructor-led certification course. Tuition is approximately $5,000 before promotions, with early bird registration offering savings of up to $2,300. Upon successful completion, graduates earn the NTA Certified PHWC (Professional Health and Wellness Coach) credential. The program requires approximately 8 to 10 hours per week and offers cohort start dates in the spring and fall. Weekly live calls are offered with two time slot options.

### Prerequisites and Admissions

The PHWC program requires a high school diploma or GED equivalent and a minimum age of 18 years. No prior health, coaching, or nutrition background is required. The program is designed to be accessible to career changers and individuals new to the wellness field, with curriculum that progressively builds coaching competency from foundational skills through advanced client practice.

### What You Learn — Curriculum Overview

The PHWC curriculum spans 8 months of progressive skill development. Months 1 and 2 build foundational coaching skills including the client-centered relationship, active listening, reflections, and goal setting. Months 3 through 5 focus on applied practice with real clients, including intake processes, initial and follow-up sessions, coaching mentor sessions, and advanced techniques like perspective shifts and positive psychology. Month 6 covers professional conduct and ethics. Month 7 integrates health promotion, chronic disease knowledge, and behavioral risk factors. Month 8 culminates in a practical skills assessment, final exam, and graduation. Five structured coaching mentor sessions are distributed throughout the program to provide supervised practice and feedback.

### What You Can Do After Graduation

PHWC graduates can work as professional health and wellness coaches in private practice, healthcare settings, corporate wellness programs, fitness industry roles, and educational institutions. PHWCs guide meaningful client behavior change, foster safe and supportive coaching environments, employ motivational interviewing for non-judgmental dialogue, assess readiness and co-create action plans, and help clients make confident, informed wellness choices. The critical distinction from NTP graduates is that PHWCs facilitate client empowerment rather than making specific food, supplement, or lifestyle recommendations.

### NBHWC Board Certification Pathway

The NTA's PHWC program is an NBHWC-approved training program, meaning graduates have completed the required educational hours for board certification. However, additional requirements must be met before sitting for the HWC Certifying Examination: completion of at least 50 documented coaching sessions, and either an associate's degree or higher or 4,000 hours of documented work experience. The NBC-HWC (National Board Certified Health and Wellness Coach) credential is a nationally recognized standard that can enhance professional credibility and expand career opportunities.

### Tuition and Financial Options

PHWC tuition is approximately $5,000, with early bird discounts and promotional pricing periodically available. The NTA offers financing through Affirm. Early bird registration has offered savings of up to $2,300 off standard tuition. The Graham Grant is not available for the PHWC program — it applies only to NTP tuition.

## Functional Clinical Assessment (FCA) Course
<!-- source_url: https://www.nutritionaltherapy.com/fca -->

### Course Summary — Duration, Cost, Prerequisite

The Functional Clinical Assessment course is a 9-week online program paired with a mandatory 3-day in-person workshop. Tuition is $2,000, which does not include travel or lodging expenses for the in-person component. The course is exclusively available to graduates of the NTP program who hold an active NTP credential — current NTP students cannot enroll, and PHWC graduates are not eligible. Enrollment is limited to approximately 22 spots per session, reflecting the hands-on nature of the training. Affirm financing is available for tuition.

### What FCA Teaches — Hands-On Palpation and Lingual-Neural Testing

The FCA course teaches nine core skill areas: body mechanics, reflex point anatomy, Lingual-Neural Testing (LNT), palpation techniques, data analysis, Coca's Pulse Test, nutrient recommendation development, and professional conduct for hands-on assessment. LNT is a technique that evaluates the body's neurological response to specific substances placed on the tongue, providing immediate bio-individual feedback about which nutrients or supplements best support a particular client. Palpation techniques allow practitioners to assess specific reflex points on the body to identify areas of organ stress and dysfunction.

### FNTP Credential Upon Completion

Successful completion of both the online coursework and the in-person workshop earns graduates the FNTP (Functional Nutritional Therapy Practitioner) credential. The FNTP designation builds upon the NTP credential, indicating that the practitioner has advanced hands-on assessment capabilities. FNTPs can perform Functional Clinical Assessments and Lingual-Neural Testing in addition to all standard NTP assessment and recommendation activities.

### In-Person Workshop Component

The 3-day in-person workshop is a mandatory component of the FCA course and cannot be waived or completed remotely. Workshops run from 8:30 AM to 5:30 PM daily. Full attendance is required to earn the FNTP credential. The workshop provides supervised practice of palpation and LNT techniques with instructor feedback. The course is led by Thomas Gilliford, FNTP, who has been a bodyworker since 2011 and an NTA instructor since 2016.

## Foundations of Healing
<!-- source_url: https://www.nutritionaltherapy.com/foundations -->

### Course Summary — 6 Weeks, Self-Paced, Personal Health

Foundations of Healing is a 6-week, self-paced online course designed for personal health improvement rather than career development. The course is priced at $500 and is hosted on NTA Connect, the organization's community platform built on Circle.so. Students have access to course materials for one year, with most participants spending 1 to 2 hours per week watching lessons and applying what they learn. One module is released per week, plus a bonus module. The course does not include direct instructor support but provides comprehensive video lessons and downloadable guides.

### Who It's For — Not Career-Focused

Foundations of Healing is designed for anyone who wants to improve their personal health through a root-cause approach. No prior nutrition knowledge is required — the course is beginner-friendly and breaks down complex health concepts into actionable, practical lessons. Typical students include individuals struggling with fatigue, digestive issues, or chronic stress, as well as people who want to prevent future health problems. This course does not lead to any professional credential or certification — it is purely for personal enrichment and self-directed health improvement.

### What You Learn

The course covers the Five Foundations of Health: nutrient-dense food, digestion, blood sugar balance, sleep, and stress management. Students learn how to create bio-individual, whole-food meal plans tailored to their unique needs. Traditional food preparation techniques are taught, including soaking, fermenting, and sprouting methods that enhance nutrient bioavailability and digestibility. The course provides practical, everyday strategies for improving health across all five foundations without requiring medical intervention or professional guidance.

## Career Compass
<!-- source_url: https://www.nutritionaltherapy.com/courses -->

### Course Summary — 10 Weeks, Business Launch

Career Compass is a 10-week online course designed to help NTA graduates launch and grow their professional practice. The course is available to graduates of both the NTP and PHWC programs. It covers the practical business aspects of running a health and wellness practice that are not included in the clinical training programs.

### Who It's For — NTA Graduates

Career Compass is specifically designed for NTA graduates who have completed their NTP or PHWC certification and are ready to transition from student to practitioner. The course addresses the common challenge that clinically skilled graduates face: knowing how to help clients but lacking the business knowledge to build a sustainable practice.

### What's Included — Legal, Branding, Marketing, NTASites

The Career Compass curriculum covers choosing a legal business structure, developing a professional brand and identity, designing service offerings and pricing, implementing client conversion strategies, and executing marketing plans for practice growth. The course includes access to NTASites, the NTA's website builder designed specifically for practitioners, and the Student Journey Content Bundle, which provides month-by-month marketing plans and content templates that graduates can customize for their practice.

## NTA Health — 1:1 Consultations
<!-- source_url: https://www.nutritionaltherapy.com/nta-health -->

### Service Overview — Work Directly with a Certified NTP

NTA Health is a direct-to-consumer service that connects individuals with certified Nutritional Therapy Practitioners for 1:1 consultations. The service is positioned as an entry point for people who want to experience nutritional therapy without enrolling in a full program. NTA Health consultations follow the same bio-individual, root-cause approach taught in the NTP curriculum, applied in a practitioner-client setting. The service is currently led by Heather McDonough, BA, FNTP, RWP, who specializes in root-cause analysis.

### What's Included — Assessment, Consultation, Digestion Guide

The NTA Health Root-Cause Health Assessment includes a detailed online assessment covering symptoms, lifestyle, nutrition, movement, stress, and sleep. A certified NTP reviews the assessment data and conducts a 1:1 consultation to identify the top hidden drivers contributing to the client's symptoms. The package includes a bonus digestion guide and an invitation to explore continued 1:1 support. The introductory assessment is priced at $25, reduced from a standard price of $100.

### Conditions Addressed

NTA Health consultations are designed to help people experiencing chronic fatigue, brain fog, digestive issues, hormone imbalances, weight management challenges, fertility concerns, autoimmune conditions, sleep disruptions, and unresolved or mystery symptoms. The service emphasizes that the approach is personalized rather than protocol-based, honoring bio-individuality in the assessment and recommendation process. NTA Health does not diagnose, treat, or prescribe — it provides nutritional therapy within the same scope of practice boundaries that apply to all NTPs.

## Nutritional Therapy 101 — Free Mini Course
<!-- source_url: https://www.nutritionaltherapy.com/courses -->

### What It Is — 5-Day Introduction

Nutritional Therapy 101 is a free 5-day mini course that introduces the NTA's nutritional therapy philosophy to prospective students and the general public. The course provides an entry-level overview of the NTA's approach to nutrition, including the concepts of bio-individuality, food-first healing, and the Five Foundations of Health. It is delivered digitally and requires no commitment beyond completing the course content over five days.

### Purpose — Entry-Level Funnel to Full Programs

Nutritional Therapy 101 serves as both an educational resource and an entry point into the NTA's program ecosystem. It allows prospective students to experience the NTA's teaching approach and philosophical framework before committing to a full certification program. The course helps potential students assess whether the NTA's holistic, bio-individualized approach to nutrition resonates with their interests and career goals.

## Comparing Programs — NTP vs PHWC vs FCA vs Foundations
<!-- source_url: https://www.nutritionaltherapy.com/courses -->

### Side-by-Side Comparison — Duration, Cost, Credential, Focus

The NTP program runs 12 months, costs approximately $8,000, earns the NTP credential, and focuses on nutrition science, the Five Foundations, and making bio-individualized dietary, supplement, and lifestyle recommendations. The PHWC program runs 8 months, costs approximately $5,000, earns the PHWC credential, and focuses on coaching psychology, behavior change facilitation, and client empowerment without making recommendations. The FCA course runs 9 weeks plus a 3-day workshop, costs $2,000, earns the FNTP credential upgrade, and focuses on hands-on assessment and Lingual-Neural Testing — it requires prior NTP completion. Foundations of Healing runs 6 weeks self-paced, costs $500, earns no credential, and focuses on personal health improvement through the Five Foundations.

### Decision Framework — Career vs Personal, Science vs Coaching

The primary decision point for prospective students is whether their goal is career development or personal health improvement. For personal health goals without career aspirations, Foundations of Healing is the appropriate choice. For career-oriented students, the next decision is between the NTP and PHWC paths. Students who are drawn to nutrition science, data analysis, and making specific recommendations should pursue the NTP. Students who are drawn to coaching psychology, behavior change, and client empowerment should pursue the PHWC. Students who want both capabilities can complete both programs sequentially. NTP graduates who want advanced hands-on assessment skills can add the FCA course to earn the FNTP credential.

### Quiz Routing Logic — How NTA Routes Prospective Students

The NTA website includes a quiz that routes prospective students to their recommended program based on their goals and preferences. Career-focused individuals who are drawn to science, data, and nutrition recommendations and can commit 15 to 20 hours per week are routed to the NTP program. Career-focused individuals who prefer behavior change, empowering others, and can commit 5 to 10 hours per week are routed to the PHWC program. Personally motivated individuals who are fairly confident and self-directed are routed to Foundations of Healing. Personally motivated individuals who want guided support and feel overwhelmed are routed to NTA Health 1:1 consultations. This routing logic is a useful framework for employees helping prospective students identify their best-fit program.
