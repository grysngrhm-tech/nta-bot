## NTA Core Philosophy
<!-- source_url: https://www.nutritionaltherapy.com/about -->

### Mission — Reverse the Trend of Preventable Poor Health

The Nutritional Therapy Association's mission is to reverse the epidemic of preventable poor health by educating and empowering wellness champions in every community. This mission statement reflects the NTA's core belief that the current health crisis is largely driven by preventable factors — primarily poor nutrition, inadequate sleep, chronic stress, and sedentary lifestyles — and that trained practitioners working at the community level are the most effective mechanism for systemic change. The NTA does not position itself as a medical institution or a replacement for conventional healthcare but rather as a complementary system that addresses the root nutritional and lifestyle causes that conventional medicine often overlooks. The phrase "wellness champions in every community" captures the NTA's distributed, grassroots theory of change.

### Vision — A Wellness Revolution and Alternative System of Care

The NTA's vision is to lead a wellness revolution that creates an alternative system of care, prioritizing people over profit with a network of professionals who provide the knowledge, resources, and support necessary to reclaim health. This vision statement positions the NTA against the current healthcare model's emphasis on treating disease after it develops, advocating instead for a proactive, prevention-oriented approach rooted in nutrition and lifestyle. The phrase "alternative system of care" does not mean replacing medical care but rather creating a parallel support structure where individuals can access personalized nutritional guidance, coaching, and education that the conventional system typically does not provide. The "people over profit" language reflects the NTA's stance against the industrialized food and pharmaceutical systems that it views as contributing to the health crisis.

### The Health Crisis NTA Exists to Address

The NTA frames its existence as a response to specific, measurable health trends that it considers preventable through nutritional intervention and lifestyle modification. The statistics the NTA cites include: 74 percent of American adults are overweight or obese, 60 percent of people are battling at least one chronic disease, one in three people have pre-diabetes, and 50 percent of children and adolescents are now overweight or obese. These statistics are used not as scare tactics but as the empirical foundation for why the NTA believes its educational mission is urgent and necessary. The organization argues that these trends are accelerating and that the conventional healthcare system, focused primarily on acute care and pharmaceutical intervention, is not equipped to reverse them. The NTA positions its practitioners as the frontline response to this crisis.

### How NTA Positions Itself Against Conventional Nutrition

The NTA explicitly differentiates its approach from conventional nutrition advice in several key ways. The NTA rejects the one-size-fits-all dietary recommendations that characterize much of mainstream nutrition guidance, instead embracing bio-individuality as a core principle. The NTA prioritizes food quality and nutrient density over calorie counting and macronutrient ratios. The NTA draws on traditional food preparation wisdom from ancestral cultures rather than relying solely on modern nutritional science. The NTA takes an omnivorous perspective rather than aligning with any specific dietary ideology such as veganism, vegetarianism, keto, or paleo. And the NTA focuses on root causes of health dysfunction rather than symptom management. This positioning is not anti-science — the NTA's primary textbook contains over 16,900 scientific references — but rather a critique of how mainstream nutrition has been simplified and industrialized at the expense of individual health.

## The Six Foundational Principles
<!-- source_url: https://www.nutritionaltherapy.com/ntp -->

### Bio-Individuality — Each Person Is Biologically Unique

Bio-individuality is the cornerstone principle of the NTA's entire philosophy. It holds that each person is biologically unique, shaped by their genetics, epigenetics, health history, environment, lifestyle, microbiome, and individual biochemistry. Because of this uniqueness, no single dietary approach, supplement protocol, or lifestyle prescription can work for everyone. The NTA explicitly states that it does not take a "one size fits all" approach to nutrition. In practice, bio-individuality means that NTPs must assess each client individually using tools like the Nutritional Assessment Questionnaire, Food and Mood Journals, and clinical interviews before making any recommendations. It means that PHWCs must tailor their coaching approach to each client's unique personality, readiness, and circumstances. Bio-individuality is sometimes listed as a sixth foundation alongside the Five Foundations of Health, reflecting its status as both a foundational principle and an overarching framework that governs how all other principles are applied.

### Food-First Approach — Rooted in Traditional Cultures

The food-first approach is the NTA's commitment to prioritizing whole, thoughtfully prepared, nutrient-dense foods as the primary intervention for health improvement. This principle holds that real food — properly sourced, prepared, and consumed — is the foundation of nutritional therapy, with supplements playing a supportive rather than primary role. The food-first approach is explicitly rooted in the preparation techniques of traditional cultures, drawing on the dietary wisdom of ancestral populations who thrived without processed foods or modern industrial agriculture. Traditional preparation methods emphasized in the NTA curriculum include fermentation, soaking, sprouting, and bone broth preparation — techniques that enhance nutrient bioavailability and digestibility. The phrase "thoughtfully prepared" is key to the NTA's framing: it is not enough to eat whole foods; how food is prepared matters as much as what food is consumed.

### Omnivorous Perspective — Not Tied to Any Single Dietary Framework

The NTA takes an explicitly omnivorous perspective on diet, meaning its curriculum and philosophy are not tied to any single dietary ideology. The NTA does not advocate for veganism, vegetarianism, keto, paleo, carnivore, or any other named dietary framework as the correct approach for all people. Instead, the omnivorous perspective holds that the ideal diet varies by individual and that human beings have historically thrived on diverse dietary patterns that included both plant and animal foods. This principle flows directly from bio-individuality: if each person is biochemically unique, then prescribing a single dietary framework for everyone contradicts the foundational philosophy. NTPs are trained to assess what dietary approach best serves each individual client rather than fitting clients into a predetermined dietary template.

### Innate Intelligence — The Body Defaults to Health

The principle of innate intelligence holds that the body possesses an inherent wisdom and defaults to health when given what it needs. This principle reflects the NTA's optimistic view of human physiology: the body is not fragile or inherently prone to disease, but rather a self-regulating, self-healing system that functions optimally when properly nourished and supported. The NTA teaches practitioners to honor the body's incredible capacity to restore, regenerate, and correct itself when provided with appropriate nutrition, adequate sleep, managed stress, and proper digestive function. Innate intelligence does not mean that the body can overcome any condition without medical intervention, but rather that supporting the body's natural healing processes through nutrition and lifestyle should be the first line of approach before assuming that external intervention is required. This principle underlies the NTA's emphasis on supporting function rather than treating disease.

### Root-Cause Focus — Why, Not Just What

The root-cause focus distinguishes the NTA's approach from symptom-based interventions. Rather than asking "what symptom needs to be suppressed," NTPs are trained to ask "why is the body responding this way." This principle holds that symptoms are the body's intelligent communication about underlying imbalances, not problems to be eliminated in isolation. For example, chronic headaches might be a symptom of dehydration, blood sugar instability, food sensitivities, or stress — each requiring a different intervention. The root-cause approach requires practitioners to dig deeper, using the Five Foundations framework to identify which foundational areas are compromised and how those compromises cascade through the body's interconnected systems. This approach often leads to interventions that address multiple symptoms simultaneously because they target the shared underlying cause rather than each symptom individually.

### Whole-Person Holistic Lens — Systems, Not Symptoms

The whole-person holistic lens teaches practitioners to support the entire body as a network of interconnected systems rather than addressing specific symptoms in isolation. This principle recognizes that the body's organs, systems, and functions do not operate independently — digestion affects nutrient absorption which affects blood sugar regulation which affects hormonal balance which affects sleep quality which affects stress resilience. A dysfunction in one area inevitably ripples through other areas. The holistic lens requires practitioners to consider the full picture of a client's health, lifestyle, emotional state, relationships, and environment when developing recommendations. This systems-thinking approach is what makes the Five Foundations framework so central to NTA education: by assessing all five foundations simultaneously, practitioners can identify the interconnected patterns that a symptom-focused approach would miss.

## Key NTA Terminology — Glossary
<!-- source_url: https://www.nutritionaltherapy.com/ntp -->

### NTP — Nutritional Therapy Practitioner

A Nutritional Therapy Practitioner is a professional who has completed the NTA's 12-month NTP certification program. NTPs are trained to evaluate nutritional needs and provide bio-individualized dietary, supplement, and lifestyle recommendations based on comprehensive client assessment. The NTP credential represents competency in the Five Foundations of Health, the NACA assessment process, and the use of clinical tools including the NAQ and Nutri-Q. NTPs can sit for the NANP National Board Exam upon graduation. NTPs cannot diagnose, treat, or prescribe — they provide nutritional education and recommendations within their defined scope of practice.

### FNTP — Functional Nutritional Therapy Practitioner

A Functional Nutritional Therapy Practitioner is an NTP who has completed the additional Functional Clinical Assessment (FCA) course. The FNTP credential indicates advanced training in hands-on assessment techniques including palpation of reflex points and Lingual-Neural Testing. FNTPs can perform Functional Clinical Assessments that provide bio-individual feedback about organ stress and optimal supplement selection. The FNTP scope includes everything in the NTP scope plus these additional assessment capabilities. The FCA course requires NTP graduation as a prerequisite and includes a mandatory 3-day in-person workshop.

### PHWC — Professional Health and Wellness Coach

A Professional Health and Wellness Coach is a professional who has completed the NTA's 8-month PHWC certification program. PHWCs are trained in coaching psychology, motivational interviewing, and behavior change facilitation. The critical distinction from NTPs is that PHWCs empower clients to make their own health decisions rather than making specific food, supplement, or lifestyle recommendations. The PHWC program is approved by the National Board for Health and Wellness Coaching (NBHWC), and graduates can pursue the nationally recognized NBC-HWC board certification after meeting additional experience requirements.

### FCA — Functional Clinical Assessment

The Functional Clinical Assessment is both a course and a technique. As a course, it is a 9-week online program plus 3-day in-person workshop available exclusively to NTP graduates. As a technique, it is a hands-on assessment method that uses targeted palpation of anatomically significant reflex points to identify areas of organ stress and dysfunction. The FCA provides bio-individual data that helps practitioners develop more precise and personalized nutritional recommendations than assessment questionnaires alone can provide.

### NAQ — Nutritional Assessment Questionnaire

The Nutritional Assessment Questionnaire is a comprehensive symptom-based survey that clients complete through the Nutri-Q platform. The NAQ systematically collects data about symptoms across all Five Foundations and body systems, generating quantitative scores that feed into the Symptom Burden Graph. The NAQ is the primary intake assessment tool for NTPs and is readministered at follow-up sessions to measure client progress over time. The questionnaire is designed to identify patterns of nutritional imbalance that guide the practitioner's NACA analysis and recommendation development.

### NACA — Nutritional Analysis and Clinical Application

The NACA process is the comprehensive framework that NTPs use to evaluate client information and create bio-individualized recommendation plans. It integrates data from the NAQ, Food and Mood Journals, lifestyle assessments, client interviews, and (for FNTPs) Functional Clinical Assessment findings into a structured analytical process. The NACA framework teaches practitioners to identify patterns across the Five Foundations, correlate symptoms with potential nutritional imbalances, and prioritize interventions based on each client's unique needs and goals. The NACA process is the core clinical methodology of the NTP credential.

### LNT — Lingual-Neural Testing

Lingual-Neural Testing is a specialized assessment technique taught exclusively in the FCA course and available only to FNTP-credentialed practitioners. LNT evaluates the body's neurological response to specific substances placed on the tongue, leveraging the tongue's dense nerve supply and its direct neurological connection to the brain. The technique provides immediate bio-individual feedback about which nutrients or supplements are most appropriate for a particular client, allowing practitioners to create highly personalized and validated supplement protocols rather than relying on generalized recommendations.

### Nutri-Q — Proprietary Client Management Software

Nutri-Q is the NTA's proprietary client management platform, designed exclusively for NTPs and PHWCs. The platform is HIPAA-compliant and provides electronic intake forms, the Nutritional Assessment Questionnaire with automated scoring, the Symptom Burden Graph visualization, the Health and Wellness Wheel Assessment (for PHWCs), progress tracking, personalized recommendation generation, and automated client reporting. Students gain access during their program and retain it after graduation. Nutri-Q is continuously updated and serves as the digital backbone of an NTA practitioner's clinical workflow.

### Symptom Burden Graph

The Symptom Burden Graph is a data visualization within Nutri-Q that displays NAQ results in a graphical format. It maps symptom severity across the Five Foundations and related body systems, providing a visual snapshot of where a client's highest symptom burdens lie. The graph serves both as a clinical prioritization tool for the practitioner and as a communication tool for client education. When readministered over time, it provides visual evidence of progress that can motivate continued client compliance.

### Health and Wellness Wheel Assessment

The Health and Wellness Wheel is a proprietary assessment tool used by PHWCs through the Nutri-Q platform. Unlike the NAQ which surveys physiological symptoms, the Wellness Wheel explores multiple dimensions of overall life satisfaction including physical health, emotional wellbeing, relationships, career, finances, spirituality, and personal growth. It provides a holistic starting point for coaching conversations and goal-setting that honors the whole person.

### Career Compass

Career Compass is a 10-week online business launch course available to NTA graduates of both the NTP and PHWC programs. It covers legal business structure, branding, service design, client conversion, and marketing. The course includes access to NTASites website builder and marketing content templates. Career Compass addresses the gap between clinical competency and business acumen that many new practitioners face.

### NTASites

NTASites is a website builder designed specifically for NTA practitioners. It provides customizable templates and integrated features for nutrition practice websites. NTASites is included with the Career Compass course and available as a standalone subscription, providing graduates with a low-barrier path to professional online presence.

### Practitioner Network

The NTA Practitioner Network is an exclusive alumni membership portal providing monthly events, educational resources, professional collaboration opportunities, advanced training webinars, and peer support. New graduates receive a free 60-day trial. The network serves as the ongoing professional community for NTA graduates after their formal education ends.

### Practitioner Directory

The Practitioner Directory is a public-facing online directory at practitionerdirectory.nutritionaltherapy.com that lists NTA graduates who opt in to being discoverable by potential clients. The directory allows people seeking nutritional therapy services to find qualified NTP, FNTP, and PHWC practitioners in their area.

### Graham Grant

The Graham Grant is a financial assistance program established in 2023 by NTA founders Joy and Gray Graham. It reduces NTP program tuition by 50 percent for U.S. residents who meet low-income thresholds defined by Office of Postsecondary Education guidelines. Applicants must submit Form 1040 tax returns for income verification. The grant cannot be combined with other promotions, is limited in supply per cohort, and is valid for 7 business days from issuance. It applies only to NTP tuition, not to the PHWC program or other courses.

## NTA Accreditation and Recognition
<!-- source_url: https://www.nutritionaltherapy.com/about -->

### Washington State Workforce Training Board — Licensed Vocational School

The NTA is licensed as a private vocational school by the Washington State Workforce Training and Education Coordinating Board. This state licensure authorizes the NTA to operate educational programs and issue credentials within Washington State's regulatory framework for vocational education. The licensure involves periodic review and compliance with state standards for vocational schools.

### NANP — National Association of Nutritional Professionals

The National Association of Nutritional Professionals has included NTA programs in its list of Approved and Recommended Holistic Nutrition Education Programs since 2012. NANP recognition means that NTP graduates are eligible to sit for the NANP National Board Exam, which is the primary board certification for holistic nutrition practitioners in the United States. NANP recognition provides external validation of the NTP curriculum's rigor and relevance to professional practice.

### NBHWC — National Board for Health and Wellness Coaching

The National Board for Health and Wellness Coaching has approved the NTA's PHWC program as an Approved Health and Wellness Coach Training and Education Program. This approval means that PHWC graduates have completed the educational requirements for NBC-HWC board certification, subject to meeting additional experience requirements. NBHWC approval is the gold standard for health coaching education programs and provides graduates with a pathway to nationally recognized board certification.

### Holistic Council Endorsement

The NTA's programs are endorsed by the Holistic Council, an organization that recognizes holistic health education programs meeting its standards. This endorsement provides additional third-party validation of the NTA's educational quality and philosophical alignment with holistic health principles.

### NOT Accredited by US Department of Education — What This Means

The NTA is not accredited by the U.S. Department of Education. This means NTA programs do not qualify for federal financial aid (FAFSA, Pell Grants, federal student loans). Credits earned at the NTA are not transferable to accredited colleges or universities. NTA credentials are professional certifications, not academic degrees. This distinction is important for prospective students who may be comparing the NTA to degree-granting institutions. The lack of DOE accreditation does not affect the validity of NTA credentials for professional practice — NTPs and PHWCs can practice within their defined scope regardless of DOE accreditation status. The NTA offers its own financing options through Affirm and the Graham Grant as alternatives to federal financial aid.

## NTA History and Timeline
<!-- source_url: https://www.nutritionaltherapy.com/about -->

### 1997 — Founded by Gray and Joy Graham

The Nutritional Therapy Association was incorporated in 1997 by Gray and Joy Graham in Olympia, Washington. The Grahams founded the NTA as a vocational nutrition school to train healthcare practitioners in nutritional therapy, filling a gap they identified in the existing nutrition education landscape. The organization was established as a private vocational school and has remained headquartered in the Olympia, Washington area throughout its history.

### 2001 — NTP Program Launched

The NTP certification program was launched in 2001, producing the first cohorts of certified Nutritional Therapy Practitioners and Functional Nutritional Therapy Practitioners. The program established the Five Foundations framework and the clinical assessment methodology that remain central to NTA education today. The launch of the NTP program represented the operationalization of the Grahams' vision for community-level nutritional therapy education.

### 2012 — NANP Recognition

In 2012, the National Association of Nutritional Professionals included NTA programs in its Approved and Recommended Holistic Nutrition Education Programs. This recognition provided external validation of the NTP curriculum and enabled graduates to sit for the NANP National Board Exam, significantly enhancing the professional credibility and career prospects of NTA graduates.

### 2023 — Graham Grant Established

In 2023, founders Joy and Gray Graham established the Graham Grant, a financial assistance program that reduces NTP tuition by 50 percent for qualifying low-income U.S. residents. The grant reflects the founders' commitment to making nutritional therapy education accessible to people from diverse economic backgrounds and their belief that financial barriers should not prevent qualified individuals from pursuing this career path.

### 2024 — PHWC Program Launched and NBHWC Approved

In 2024, the NTA launched its Professional Health and Wellness Coach (PHWC) program, marking the organization's expansion beyond nutritional therapy into coaching psychology and behavior change. The program received approval from the National Board for Health and Wellness Coaching, enabling graduates to pursue nationally recognized NBC-HWC board certification. The PHWC launch represented a strategic diversification of the NTA's educational offerings and an acknowledgment that sustainable health improvement requires both nutrition expertise and behavior change support.

### 2025 and Beyond — Legislative Advocacy and Expansion

The NTA has committed to legislative advocacy and educational expansion as ongoing priorities. This includes engaging with state-level regulatory processes that affect the practice of nutritional therapy, advocating for practitioner recognition in jurisdictions where regulations may be unclear, and continuing to develop new educational offerings that serve both practitioners and the general public. The organization frames its future as one of continued growth in scale and impact, driven by its founding mission to reverse the trend of preventable poor health.

## NTA Impact Awards
<!-- source_url: https://www.nutritionaltherapy.com/about -->

### Community Impact Award — Criteria and Process

The Community Impact Award is one of two annual awards recognizing NTA graduates who demonstrate exceptional impact. Three graduates are selected each year for using their NTA-developed skillset to make a meaningful difference in their communities. The selection process is subjective and application-based, with an application window typically in August and announcements in December. Applicants describe their community contributions and how their NTA training has enabled their impact work. This award celebrates graduates who embody the NTA's mission of reversing poor health at the community level through education, advocacy, and direct service.

### Clinical Impact Award — Nutri-Q Data-Driven Selection

The Clinical Impact Award recognizes three graduates each year who excel in clinical practice, as objectively measured through Nutri-Q platform data. Unlike the subjective Community Impact Award, the Clinical Impact Award is determined by quantitative metrics — specifically, the total number of NAQs or Wellness Wheel Assessments issued annually through Nutri-Q. No application is required; practitioners are automatically evaluated based on their platform usage data. This award incentivizes active clinical practice and consistent use of the NTA's assessment tools while recognizing practitioners who maintain high client volumes and thorough assessment practices.

## Free Resources and Lead Magnets

### Nutritional Therapy 101 — Free 5-Day Mini Course

Nutritional Therapy 101 is a free 5-day digital mini course that introduces the NTA's nutritional therapy philosophy to prospective students and the general public. The course covers the foundational concepts of bio-individuality, food-first healing, and the Five Foundations of Health in an accessible, no-commitment format. It serves as both an educational resource and an entry point into the NTA's program ecosystem, allowing prospective students to experience the NTA's teaching style and philosophical approach before committing to a paid certification program.

### Downloadable Guides — Healthy Fats, Herbal Recipes, Digestion, Stress Management

The NTA offers several free downloadable guides as educational resources and lead generation tools. Available guides include a Healthy Fats Guide, an Herbal Recipes guide, a Journal for Self Growth, a Stress Management Guide, a Digestion Guide (which also serves as the bonus included with NTA Health consultations), and a Nutrient-Dense Swaps Mini Guide. These guides provide practical, actionable content consistent with the NTA's philosophy and serve to introduce the NTA's approach to nutrition and wellness to a broad audience.

### Which Path Is Right for You Quiz — Routing Logic

The NTA website features an interactive quiz that helps prospective students identify their best-fit program based on their goals, preferences, and circumstances. The quiz routes respondents to one of four pathways: career-focused individuals oriented toward science, data, and making nutrition recommendations are directed to the NTP Program; career-focused individuals oriented toward behavior change and client empowerment are directed to the PHWC Program; personally motivated individuals who are self-directed and confident are directed to Foundations of Healing; and personally motivated individuals who want guided support and feel overwhelmed are directed to NTA Health 1:1 consultations. This routing logic is a useful decision framework for NTA employees helping prospective students navigate program selection.
